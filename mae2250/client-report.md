# Client Report: Spotted Lantern Fly Trap

## Project Overview

**Team:** Team 003  
**Clients:** Cornell CALS Extension / E&J Gallo Winery / National Grape  
**Members:** Lydia Woodall, Ben Sedran, Charlotte Huber, Esmeralda Bernal Martinez, Lisa Chen  

---

## Problem Context

Spotted lantern fly (SLF) infestations pose a major threat to vineyards across the United States, causing up to **$8.8 million in damages over three years** due to vine damage, reduced fruit quality, and product contamination.

Even small numbers of SLFs can contaminate an entire harvest, leading to rejected product loads. During harvest, SLFs cluster on grapevines and are collected along with the fruit. At this stage, **pesticide use is not viable due to food safety concerns**, and manual removal is impractical at scale.

Currently, there is **no effective mechanical system** to draw SLFs away from vines and concentrate them into a controlled location during harvest.

---

## Proposed Solution

We developed a **low-cost mechanical trap** designed to attract, capture, and contain SLFs away from grapevines during harvest.

### System Components

- **Funnel-Flap Entry System**  
  Allows SLFs to enter easily but prevents escape  

- **Container with Sliding Drawer**  
  Collects SLFs and stores attractant liquid  

- **Adjustable Pole**  
  Positions the trap at optimal height (6–9 ft)

SLFs are attracted using a natural attractant (tree-of-heaven sap). Once inside, they pass through a funnel that opens under small force but closes to prevent escape.

## How It Works

1. SLFs are attracted to the trap using natural sap  
2. They enter through the funnel-flap mechanism  
3. Flaps open under small force (~0.05 N)  
4. Flaps close behind them, preventing escape  
5. SLFs are collected in a removable drawer  

---

## Testing & Results

### Stability Test
- **Test:** Pole inserted ~1 ft into soil and shaken  
- **Result:** System was unstable → requires deeper anchoring  

---

### Drawer Load Test
- **Test:** Loaded with 5.4 kg and cycled 10 times  
- **Result:** Operated smoothly with no deformation  

---

### Funnel Performance Test
- **Test:** Model insect pulled through funnel  
- **Result:**  
  - Opens at ~0.05 N  
  - Prevents escape once inside  

👉 Indicates real SLFs likely cannot escape after entry  

---

## Key Performance Metrics

- Funnel opening force: **~0.05 N**  
- Drawer capacity: **up to 12 kg**  
- Total supported load: **~14.25 kg**  

---

## Design Details

- **Drawer:**  
  - Wheel-based sliding system for easy removal  
  - Supports high loads without jamming  

- **Funnel:**  
  - Octagonal one-way valve with adjustable flaps  
  - Rubber band provides restoring force  
  - Hard stops prevent overextension  

- **Container:**  
  - Laser-cut acrylic housing  
  - Removable base for easy cleaning/refilling  

- **Pole:**  
  - Telescoping steel rails  
  - Adjustable height (6–9 ft)

## Conclusion

The prototype successfully demonstrates a **mechanically viable solution** for SLF capture and containment.

- Funnel effectively prevents escape  
- Drawer operates reliably under load  
- System functions as an integrated design  

---

## Recommendations & Next Steps

To prepare for real-world deployment:

- Improve pole stability (e.g., screw-in anchors or wider base)  
- Add weather protection (rain/debris cover)  
- Refine funnel alignment and durability  
- Reduce reliance on manual funnel adjustments  

With these improvements, the system could be scaled for **practical vineyard use**.
