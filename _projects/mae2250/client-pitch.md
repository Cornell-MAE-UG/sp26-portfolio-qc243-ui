## Problem Statement

Vineyards across the United States are increasingly impacted by spotted lantern fly (SLF) infestations. According to reporting from *Cornell Chronicle*, vineyards can experience up to $8.8 million in losses over three years due to vine death, reduced fruit quality, and product contamination.

Currently, there is no mechanical system that attracts SLFs away from grapevines during the harvest window and concentrates them in a controlled location for removal or containment.

---

## Why It Matters to the End User

During harvest, insects present on vines are collected with the fruit. Even a small number of SLFs can contaminate an entire load, resulting in rejection. At this stage, pesticides cannot be applied, and manual removal is impractical.

A system that removes SLFs during harvest would reduce rejected loads without disrupting existing harvesting operations.

---

## Proposed Directions

### Concept A: Directional Light & Vibration SLF Attraction Station

**Description**  
A mounted station placed a fixed distance from the vine uses directional light and mechanically transmitted vibration to draw SLFs away from the crop and into a passive funnel for containment.

**How It Would Be Used**
- Installed at row ends or vineyard edges  
- Periodically emptied and repositioned based on SLF activity  

**Advantages Over Current Methods**
- Usable during harvest when pesticides and manual removal are not viable  
- Reduces SLFs on fruit without slowing harvest operations  

**End-of-Semester Proof of Concept**  
A prototype featuring adjustable light angle, vibration mount, and funnel system to measure SLF clustering relative to vine distance and capture rate.

---

### Concept B: Scent and Visual Cue SLF Trap

**Description**  
A cylindrical trap with inward-angled entry holes sized for SLFs. The trap uses the scent of Tree of Heaven and a tall, slender silhouette to attract insects. Inside, a liquid inhibits flight and drowns the SLFs.

**How It Would Be Used**
- Hung or mounted on poles above or below vines  
- Passively attracts and kills SLFs during harvest  

**Advantages Over Current Methods**
- Usable during harvest instead of pesticides or manual removal  
- Requires minimal labor  
- Actively removes SLFs from the crop before harvest  

**End-of-Semester Proof of Concept**  
A cylindrical trap with inward-angled openings, internal scent source (Tree of Heaven sap), and a liquid basin (e.g., soapy water) to disorient and drown SLFs.

---

## Key Risks / Unknowns

**Risk 1**  
The quantitative effectiveness of light, vibration, or Tree of Heaven scent in attracting SLFs is unknown.  
*Mitigation:* Test in a controlled environment with SLFs given access to both vines and stimuli.

**Risk 2**  
Potential effects on non-target organisms and variability due to weather or time of day.  
*Mitigation:* Conduct controlled trials under varying environmental conditions.

## Questions for Clients

- **Trap Type & Placement:** Where can a trap be installed without interfering with the mechanical harvester?  
- **Electricity Needs:** If power is required, should the trap use a battery or an external source such as a solar panel?  
- **Attracting SLF:** What effective and practical bait options are available to attract SLFs into the trap?
